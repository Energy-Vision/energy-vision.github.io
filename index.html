At present, Dr. Hong Xu is a Visiting Research Fellow at the Oxford Institute of Energy Studies in the UK, researching Energy Economics and Policies in the EU and China. He is serving also as a Reviewer for several highly regarded journals in the Energy and Transport fields. Dr. Xu chaired a session entitled "Chinese Energy Transition and Technology Innovation" at the United Nations Bonn Climate Change Conference, and contributed his expertise as an Academic Jury member at the St. Gallen Symposium, a historical Swiss Think-tank. As an interdisciplinary scholar, Dr. Xu's research interests extend across multiple sectors, including Clean Energy, Functional Materials, Energy Economics and Policies, Low-carbon Transport, and Digital Sustainability.

He received his PhD with research focus on Renewable Energy from ETH Zurich in 2021, and engaged in 2 years Industrial and 4 years Academic collaborative projects for R&D of automotive hydrogen fuel cells at the Energy and Environment Division of Paul Scherrer Institute, a Swiss Federal Research Institution. In 2016, he obtained EU dual master degrees in Materials Science from the Technical University of Munich, and Physics from the University of Rennes I, alongside industrial practices at European XFEL and Infineon in Germany and training at ENPC ParisTech in France. He went to university at a young age of 16, and he finished the bachelor studies at Beijing Transport University with 3 patents granted in 2013. In parallel to academic journey, he developed professional education as a part-time MBA fellow at the Quantic School of Business with practical experience at dual-currency Venture Capital firm and top Securities Company in China.
